A correlation design is a study that is used to investigate how two variables are related to each other. The strengths of this design is that it can measure many variables. The limitations of this design is that it cannot identify a casual relationship. An experimental design, like a correlational design can examine the relationship between variables, but is limited in regards that it can only test a few variables. A descriptive study can measure many variables as well, but is unable to test a relationship between said variables. A psychology question that would fit best with a correlational design study is one in which we're interested in how two variables are related opposed to a casual relationship.A psychological question that can be best answered with a correlational design is language and perception. Let's say that I am a researcher who's interested in seeing how language affects people's perception of a speaker. A descriptive study would not be a good method for my study because it is unable to test the relationship between my two variables. An experimental study would not be a good method either because it narrows the study down to a few variables, and my question will require to think about a variety of intersectional disciplines and concepts. In a correlational study I can survey people and see how they change their diction based on the perceptions of others, and additionally test to see how people's perception of others is contingent on language. A correlational study would allow me to take into consideration culture, the hegemony, and power relations.

Metacognition is defined as the intrinsic awareness of one's own thinking. Being aware of our own thinking (metacognition) is important for our development as students and people, as it brings to light our strengths and weaknesses. Knowing how we think can improve the methods in which we study. There are many different learning techniques, but one of the most effective techniques are Bloom's taxonomy and a study cycle! Bloom's taxonomy is a series of steps that we must take to truly learn new concepts. Bloom's taxonomy is effective because it moves past study strategies that emphasize memorization. Instead the taxonomy suggests that to learn a new material we need to move upstream. First we need to build a foundation (definitions and simple explanations), then we use our foundations to draw new connections, and finally we use what we know to create. Using Bloom's taxonomy is a form of metacognition. We can use this learning technique to guide how we study. For instance if I want to move up from evaluate to create I can target my learning to become more creative. In the evaluating stage I can use concept mapping or compare and contrast maps to emphasize critical thinking and argumentation.
